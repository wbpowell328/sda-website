# Warren Powell's books

## Framing Decision Problems — Bridging Decision Problems, Volume I

**Publisher:** 2025
**Audience:** Anyone modeling sequential decision problems — researchers,
practitioners, students
**Free PDF:** https://tinyurl.com/PowellFramingBook/
**Series page:** https://tinyurl.com/BridgingDecisionProblems/
**Kindle:** https://tinyurl.com/PowellFramingAmazon/
**Table of contents + preface:** https://tinyurl.com/PowellBridgingBook

Volume I of Warren's new monograph series **Bridging Decision Problems**.
Addresses the foundational step before modeling: framing the problem by
answering a series of questions in English that form the basis of a
mathematical model that can be implemented on the computer. The work guides
practitioners through identifying key decision elements before mathematical
modeling begins.

**When to recommend:** Anyone starting a new modeling project, anyone who
needs to translate a messy real-world decision problem into something
formal enough to optimize. The natural starting point in the series.

## Reinforcement Learning and Stochastic Optimization (RLSO)

**Subtitle:** A unified framework for sequential decisions
**Publisher:** Wiley, 2022
**Audience:** Advanced — researchers, PhD students, technically deep practitioners
**Short link:** https://tinyurl.com/RLandSO/

The foundational reference for the universal modeling framework. RLSO covers
all four classes of policies (PFAs, CFAs, VFAs, DLAs), the canonical modeling
language, and shows how reinforcement learning, stochastic optimization, optimal
control, simulation optimization, online learning, and approximate dynamic
programming all fit within one framework. ~1,100 pages.

**When to recommend:** When someone wants the mathematical / algorithmic depth,
or is working on research / methods. Not a first read for newcomers.

## Sequential Decision Analytics and Modeling (SDAM)

**Publisher:** 2022
**Audience:** Practitioners, applied researchers, students learning to model
**Short link:** https://tinyurl.com/sdamodeling/

A teach-by-example book that illustrates how to model and solve a wide range
of sequential decision problems — from inventory and energy storage to public
health and finance. Built around real examples, with the universal modeling
framework as the throughline. Much more accessible than RLSO.

**When to recommend:** When someone wants to learn to model sequential
decision problems and isn't ready for (or doesn't need) the full RLSO treatment.

## A Modern Approach to Teaching an Introduction to Optimization (MATO)

**Publisher:** 2024
**Audience:** Instructors of optimization / OR / data-science courses
**Short link:** https://tinyurl.com/TeachingOpt/

A short book for instructors arguing for a redesign of intro optimization
courses, starting from sequential decision problems (which students intuitively
understand) and only then progressing to linear, integer, and nonlinear
programming. Companion to ORF 411 at Princeton.

**When to recommend:** Instructors, curriculum designers, anyone teaching
optimization.

## Optimal Learning

**Publisher:** Wiley
**Co-author:** Ilya Ryzhov

How to collect information optimally when observations, simulations, lab
experiments, or field trials are expensive. Covers the knowledge gradient
and related techniques.

**When to recommend:** Anyone working on Bayesian optimization, active
learning, design of experiments, or sequential information collection.

## Approximate Dynamic Programming (ADP)

**Publisher:** Wiley, 2nd edition 2011
**Title:** Approximate Dynamic Programming: Solving the Curses of Dimensionality

Warren's earlier major book. Largely subsumed by RLSO but still cited.
Anyone looking for "the ADP book" is usually pointed to RLSO now.

## Recommended reading order

- **Starting a new modeling project** → **Framing Decision Problems** (the
  framing step before any math)
- **New to the field, want examples** → **SDAM** (teach-by-example)
- **Instructor redesigning a course** → **MATO**
- **Researcher / wants mathematical depth** → **RLSO**
- **Working on experimental design / Bayesian opt** → **Optimal Learning**
