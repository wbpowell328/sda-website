# Warren B. Powell — biography and current role

Warren B. Powell is **Professor Emeritus** in the Department of Operations
Research and Financial Engineering (ORFE) at Princeton University, where
he founded and directed **CASTLE Lab** (Computational and Stochastic Transportation,
Logistics, and Energy) from 1990 until his retirement on September 1, 2020.
He is currently **Chief Innovation Officer at Optimal Dynamics**, a company
that licensed his work on truckload trucking and dynamic resource allocation.

## Awards

- 2022 — Saul Gass Expository Writing Award (across all INFORMS subdivisions)
- 2024 — Daniel H. Wagner Prize finalist (CASTLE Lab work)

## Research focus

Warren's work centers on **sequential decision problems under uncertainty** —
problems where decisions must be made over time as new information arrives,
recognizing that decision impacts are uncertain. His major intellectual
contribution is a **universal modeling framework** that organizes all
sequential decision problems around a small number of components:

- **State variables** — what we know at each point in time
- **Decision variables** — what we choose
- **Exogenous information** — what arrives that we don't control
- **Transition function** — how the system evolves
- **Objective function** — what we're optimizing

Within this framework, all policies (methods for making decisions) fall into
one of **four classes**: policy function approximations (PFAs), cost function
approximations (CFAs), value function approximations (VFAs), and direct
lookahead policies (DLAs) — plus hybrids of these four.

## Application areas

- **Energy** — PENSA (Princeton Laboratory for Energy Systems Analysis):
  storage, stochastic unit commitment, bidding, pricing, renewable integration
- **Transportation & logistics** — fleet management in trucking, rail, and
  air (work dating to 1981); real-time dispatching, routing, scheduling,
  spare parts
- **Optimal learning** — when observations and experiments are expensive
- **Health sciences** — drug discovery and delivery, blood management,
  dosage decisions, health policy
- **Public safety** — storm and wildfire response
