<p>Over my 39 years of teaching at Princeton, I realized after I retired that I had taught 16 different lecture courses, plus two graduate seminars.  I sometimes re-used a course number, but I only count it as a different course if there was a new title and entirely new content. Only four of the courses used an existing textbook.  I wrote four books for four other courses.</p>
<!-- /wp:tadv/classic-paragraph -->

<li>CIV411->ORF 411 – Departmental capstone course: Operations and Information Engineering (1996- 2016)- Required departmental capstone course for seniors. The course featured the theme of "dynamic resource management" where "resources" could be physical, financial or informational. A major feature of this version of the capstone course was the "Orange Juice Game" - a highly competitive business game that became quite well-known on Wall St.</li>

<li>ORF 544 – Stochastic Optimization and Learning (2016-2019) - I wrote an 1100 page book, <a href="https://tinyurl.com/RLandSO/">Reinforcement Learning and Stochastic Optimization</a>, that unifies 15 distinct fields for making decisions under uncertainty. In its third year, the course attracted 50 students from 10 different departments.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li>ORF 411 – Sequential Decision Analytics and Modeling (2018) - The book I wrote for this course (which required shutting down the capstone also taught under this number) provides a universal framework for sequential decision problems at an undergraduate level (see <a href="https://tinyurl.com/sdamodeling">https://tinyurl.com/sdamodeling</a>). This book has been downloaded over 15,000 times.</li>
