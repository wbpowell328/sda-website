What we do

Since its establishment in 1990, CASTLE Lab has worked on a wide range of problems sharing one characteristic: making decisions over time as new information is arriving. Decisions have to be made recognizing that their impact is uncertain.

Using a laboratory-industry partnership, our focus has been modeling first, then developing algorithms to make effective decisions under uncertainty. This led to a universal framework that optimizes over policies — methods for making decisions — with any policy falling in one of four classes.

We start every problem by answering three questions: what are the performance metrics, what types of decisions are being made, and what are the sources of uncertainty?

Application laboratories

  • PENSA — Energy systems analysis: storage, stochastic unit commitment, bidding, pricing.
  • Transportation & Logistics — Fleet management, real-time dispatching, routing, spare parts, since 1981.
  • Optimal Learning — Collecting information when experiments are expensive.
  • Health sciences — Drug discovery and delivery, blood management, dosing, health policy.
