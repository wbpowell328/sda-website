What we do

Since its establishment in 1990, CASTLE Lab has worked on a wide range of problems that share a common characteristic: making decisions (sometimes very complex decisions) over time as new information is arriving. This means decisions have to be made recognizing that their impact is uncertain.

Using a unique laboratory-industry partnership, the primary focus of our work has been modeling, followed by the development of algorithms to make effective decisions under uncertainty. This led to the universal modeling framework which focuses on optimizing over policies (methods for making decisions). Any policy falls in one of four classes which are chosen based on a number of characteristics.

We start every problem by answering three questions: what are the performance metrics, what types of decisions are being made, and what are the sources of uncertainty?

Laboratories & focus areas

  • PENSA — Princeton Laboratory for Energy Systems Analysis. Energy storage, stochastic unit commitment, bidding, pricing.
  • Transportation & Logistics — Stochastic fleet management in trucking, rail, and air; real-time dispatching, routing, spare parts.
  • Optimal Learning — Collecting information when observations, simulations, and experiments are expensive.
  • Health sciences — Drug discovery, drug delivery, blood management, dosage decisions, health policy.
